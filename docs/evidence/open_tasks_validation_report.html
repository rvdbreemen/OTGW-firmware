Where it stands

The bug this task was actually filed for — the device wedging permanently under load — is fixed and proven with real overload testing. What's left (AC#3/#4) is pure performance polish: fewer requests, smaller transfers. Neither is a broken thing to fix; both are new build-pipeline surface with a real trade-off to weigh.

The actual decision

CSS bundling (AC#3): ds-tokens.css is shared by four pages, not just v2. Bundling it into v2.css means either (a) a build-time concatenation step in build.py — clean, but touches the build pipeline — or (b) hand-copying the CSS variables into v2.css, which drifts out of sync the next time someone edits ds-tokens.css.

Gzip (AC#4): the serving code already supports it — webSendFile(path, type, gzip=true) just needs pre-compressed .gz files in the LittleFS image and an Accept-Encoding check per route. Neither piece exists yet; both are a build.py change plus route rewiring.

How to close it

Option A — ship as-is: the wedge is fixed, that was the point of the task. Tell me to close AC#3/#4 as "won't do" (or spin them into a separate, lower-priority perf task) and I'll mark 989 Done today.

Option B — go for the polish: tell me to build the concatenation step + gzip pipeline. It's real work (a build.py change, a route change, a fresh ramp to confirm it helps) but nothing I can't do — just wanted your sign-off before touching the build pipeline again this session.

TASK-994

Research: minimize BLE's internal-DRAM footprint

Your call on the payoff
  • ✓#1 6 hypotheses documented with pros/cons
  • ✓#2 Test plan adversarially reviewed (by an independent agent, findings incorporated)
  • #3 On-device evidence for the untested hypotheses
  • ✓#4 Implementation proposal with 3 options
  • #5 Your decision on which option (if any) to pursue

Where it stands

The research is done and independently stress-tested — the adversarial review actually caught two overstated claims (fixed: the PSRAM hypothesis was more constrained than first written, and the "lazy init" precedent for controller teardown turned out not to exist in the codebase at all). A fresh on-device reading also replaced the stale baseline this task was opened against: the real gap today is roughly 35–40KB, not the originally reported 64KB, because the NimBLE trim from a related fix already closed most of it.

The actual decision

Three options, in docs/evidence/task994_ble_dram_hypotheses.md:

  1. A — do nothing further. The remaining gap is small and the symptom it was chasing (unstyled UI under load) is already fixed by other work this session. My recommendation.
  2. B — lazy BLE controller init/deinit. Reclaims the full remaining gap during idle windows, but it's a from-scratch feature (not an extension of anything existing) and needs its own stability soak before shipping.
  3. C — PSRAM-backed BLE allocations. Highest theoretical payoff, but NimBLE ships as a precompiled library on this toolchain, so the config knob that would enable this may not even be reachable without forking the framework. Likely not worth it.

How to close it

Tell me A, B, or C. A closes the task today (nothing left to build). B or C become new backlog tasks with their own scope — I'd write those up once you pick.
